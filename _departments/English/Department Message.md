<p>The English Language Department aims to develop SKLites who are confident
and competent communicators in internationally acceptable English, who
are engaged, thoughtful and purposeful users of the language. Through a
balanced and progressive approach, SKLites are provided with meaningful
opportunities to think critically, express themselves clearly and connect
with others.</p>
<p>Anchored in the English Language Syllabus 2020 and the STELLAR 2.0 programme,
our curriculum emphasises the development of strong language foundations
alongside the ability to make meaning across different contexts and modes.
Students engage with a range of texts and learning experiences that build
their skills in listening, reading, viewing, speaking, writing, grammar
and vocabulary, while fostering creativity, interaction and a love for
the language.</p>
<p>To complement this, our Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in Enhanced Spoken
English, guided by the SPEAK framework (Speak, Perform, Engage, Acknowledge
and Know), provides authentic platforms such as storytelling, drama and
public speaking. These experiences enable SKLites to develop confidence
in their voice, deepen their thinking, and communicate effectively with
purpose and audience awareness.</p>
<p>Through these programmes, SKLites grow to become empathetic communicators,
discerning readers and critical thinkers, equipped with the skills and
dispositions to navigate an increasingly complex and interconnected world.</p>
