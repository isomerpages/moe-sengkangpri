<p><strong>Curriculum</strong>
</p>
<p>The Lifeskills curriculum adopts a storytelling and inquiry-based approach
to deepen students’ understanding of the school values—Respect, Integrity,
Care, and Excellence. Weekly alignment between Value Moments and Lifeskills
lessons ensures a coherent focus on a common value message, reinforcing
consistency in learning.</p>
<p>At the end of each term, students engage in self- and peer-assessment
using rubrics in their Lifeskills booklets. Aligned with the HDP “I Will”
statements, these tools support students in reflecting on their growth
and demonstration of values. Students also affirm their peers by presenting
cut-out certificates to those who exemplify the values, fostering a culture
of recognition and appreciation.</p>
<p>Through Lifeskills lessons, students are introduced to Design Thinking
as a problem-solving approach. By considering issues from the perspective
of the end-user, students develop empathy, think critically, and build
confidence—growing into thinkers today and leaders tomorrow.</p>
